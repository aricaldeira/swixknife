3. Units of Length, Speed, Acceleration, Area and Volume

The unit of length is the pada (symbol pd), defined as the path travelled by light in a vacuum during a time interval of 1⁄1352,5510,4032 1⁄599,584,916 of an agrima.

From the pada and the agrima, we define the following coherent derived units:

  • 1 vega (symbol vg) is a speed of 1 pada per 1 agrima (pd · agm−1; pd / agm), or 1 pada times 1 avrita (pd · vrt);
  • 1 tevara (symbol tvr) is an acceleration of 1 pada per 1 square agrima (pd · agm−2; pd / agm2), or 1 pada times 1 square avrita (pd · vrt2);
  • 1 keshe (symbol ksh) is an area of 1 square pada (pd2);
  • 1 aytan (symbol ayt) is a volume of 1 cubic pada (pd3);

Pay attention that, for the square and cubic units, prefixes act on the unit itself, so that:

  • 1 Cksh / 4ksh = 1 Dpd2 / 2pd2 = 1,0000 ksh = 1,0000 pd2,
    but 1 Cpd2 / 4pd2 = 1 EDksh / 12ksh = 1,0000,0000 ksh = 1,0000,0000 pd2
  • 1 cksh / 4ksh = 1 dpd2 / 2pd2 = 0.0001 ksh = 0.0001 pd2,
    but 1 cpd2 / 4pd2 = edksh / 12ksh = 0.0000 0001 ksh = 0.0000 0001 pd2
  • 1 Cayt / 4ayt = 10 Epd3 ¹pd3 = 1,0000 ayt = 1,0000 pd3,
    but 1 Cpd3 4pd3 = 1 DSayt 20ayt = 1,0000,0000,0000 ayt = 1,0000,0000,0000 pd3
  • 1 cayt / 4ayt = 0.1 epd3 / 1pd3 = 0.0001 ayt = 0.0001 pd3,
    but 1 cpd3 / 4pd3 = 1 dsayt / 20ayt = 0.0000 0000 0001 ayt = 0.0000 0000 0001 pd3

For civil or day-to-day measure of volume, it may be more convenient to deal with a smaller, even though non-coherent, unit:

  • 1 varti (symbol vrt) is a volume of 1 chatiaytan / cayt / 4ayt = 0.0001 0.000 7 7̅1̅6̅ ̅0̅4̅9̅ ̅3̅8̅2̅ aytan, that is equal to 0.0001 0.000 7 7̅1̅6̅ ̅0̅4̅9̅ ̅3̅8̅2̅ pd3.
