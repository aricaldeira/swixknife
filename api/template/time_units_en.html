Shastadari Time Units

Shastadari’s fundamental time unit is the anuga, described on the Base Units page;

Civil time (as in time on a clock) is divided into six units; one day is divided regularly into sixths, and we group those sixths into units as such:

  • the dina (DEE-nuh), symbol din, corresponding to day itself;
  • the uta (OO-tuh), symbol uta, corresponding to the first two divisions, closer to the hour (0󱹮4 h = 104 min; 0‥6 h = 40 min);
  • the posha (POH-shuh), symbol pox, corresponding to the next two divisions, closer to the minute (1󱹮04 min / 1‥1 min);
  • the agrima (uh-GREE-muh), symbol agm, corresponding to the next two divisions, closer to the second (1󱹮504 s / 1‥851 s);
  • the anuga (uh-NOO-guh), symbol ang, corresponding to the next two divisions, closer to the centisecond, the base unit of time already described (5󱹮041󱹭04 cs / ~ 5.144 032 922 cs);
  • the boda (BOH-duh), symbol bod, corresponding to the last two divisions, closer to the millisecond (1󱹮232󱹭350󱹬4 ms / ~ 1.428 898 034 ms);

So, one day is divided:

5󱹮55:55:55:55:55

   5 󱹮  55 :    55 :     55 :    55 :   55

dina 󱹮 uta : posha : agrima : anuga : boda

The name of each of the units come from the following Sanskrit words:

  • दिन ‹dina› /‍'d̪i.nə‍/: Day, cognate with Latin diēs;
  • उत्थानम् ‹utthānam› /‍ut̪'t̪ʰɑː.nəm‍/: Rising up;
  • पोषण ‹poṣaṇa› /‍'poː.ʂə.ɳə‍/: Fostering;
  • अग्रिम ‹agrima› /‍ə'gri.mə‍/: Preceding;
  • अनुगामी ‹anugāmī› /‍ə.nu'gɑː.miː‍/: Following;
  • बोध ‹bodha› /‍'boː.d̪ʰə‍/: Knowledge;
